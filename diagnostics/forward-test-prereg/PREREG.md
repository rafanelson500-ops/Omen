## Background

OMEN is a GEX-based flow-burst strategy trading ES futures on 5-minute
bars. After applying three bug fixes (session-boundary contamination,
time-stop off-by-one, trade overlap prevention), the honest performance
on the consumed 160-session corpus is:

  IS Sharpe: +2.57 (257 trades, 74 sessions)
  OOS Sharpe: +0.51 (247 trades, 72 sessions)

Two hypotheses emerged from cell-breakdown analysis on the consumed
corpus. Both are hindsight-derived — the hypotheses were generated by
observing which cells performed best and worst on OOS data. This
pre-registration locks the hypotheses, decision criteria, and stopping
rules before any forward-session data is analyzed.

## Hypothesis 1: OMEN-minus-SHORT_long (primary)

### Statement
Restricting OMEN to three cells (LONG_long, LONG_short, SHORT_short)
by excluding all SHORT_long signals will produce materially better
risk-adjusted returns than full OMEN on forward sessions.

### Mechanism
SHORT_long = OMEN going SHORT in long-gamma regime. Long-gamma dealers
are forced to buy dips mechanically. OMEN shorting into that regime
means betting on a down move while the largest market participants are
programmatically buying every decline. This creates a structural
headwind that should persist regardless of market regime.

The finding replicates across all three bug-fix iterations:
  Buggy OOS: SL Sharpe -1.95 (n=48)
  Bugfixed OOS: SL Sharpe -1.70 (n=68)
  Fresh sessions (n=2): directionally consistent

### Decision criteria (BOTH must pass for PASS verdict)
  PRIMARY: OMEN-minus-SL forward Sharpe >= 1.20
  SECONDARY: OMEN-minus-SL forward Sharpe > full-OMEN forward Sharpe
             by at least 0.50 Sharpe points

  Marginal zone (defaults to FAIL unless secondary is strong):
    OMEN-minus-SL forward Sharpe 0.80-1.19

  FAIL: OMEN-minus-SL forward Sharpe < 0.80
        OR full OMEN beats minus-SL on forward sessions
        OR SHORT_long is NOT the worst-performing cell on forward data

### Sample size
  Minimum 30 fresh sessions before any verdict
  Expected trades in minus-SL arm: ~38 trades per 30 sessions
  (based on ~1.25 trades/session minus ~0.23 SHORT_long trades/session)

### Stopping rules
  Early STOP if: after 20+ fresh sessions, SHORT_long shows positive
  Sharpe on forward data AND OMEN-minus-SL shows no improvement over
  full OMEN. This would mean the cell-breakdown finding was hindsight
  selection and the hypothesis is likely false.

  Continue to 60 sessions if: result is in marginal zone at 30 sessions.

  PASS or FAIL declared at 30-60 sessions per criteria above.

## Hypothesis 2: LS-only (secondary, parallel)

### Statement
Restricting OMEN to LONG_short signals only (long entry in short-gamma
regime) will produce Sharpe > 1.0 on forward sessions as a standalone
strategy.

### Mechanism
LONG_short = OMEN going LONG in short-gamma regime. Short-gamma dealers
are forced to buy rallies (delta-hedge their short-gamma exposure by
buying as price rises). OMEN going long in this regime means betting
on an up move while dealers mechanically amplify any up move. This
creates structural tailwind — OMEN and dealer flow are aligned.

The finding across all windows:
  Buggy IS: +5.05 (n=45)
  Buggy OOS: +2.07 (n=29)
  Bugfixed IS: +3.00 (n=46)
  Bugfixed OOS: +2.37 (n=45)
  Fresh sessions: positive direction (n=9, too small for Sharpe)

IS→OOS degradation: ~40% (vs 80% for full strategy). Most stable cell.

### Decision criteria
  PASS: LS-only forward Sharpe >= 1.00 on minimum 30 LS trades
  MARGINAL: 0.60 - 0.99 (inconclusive, requires 50+ trades)
  FAIL: < 0.60 OR negative

### Sample size
  LS fires ~0.58 trades per session on historical data.
  To accumulate 30 LS trades: ~52 fresh sessions (~2.5 months).
  This is the binding constraint — longer than Hypothesis 1.

  Both hypotheses tracked in parallel on the same fresh sessions.
  Same sessions, no additional data cost.

### Stopping rules
  Cannot declare verdict before 30 LS-specific trades.
  If after 60 sessions LS hasn't fired 30 times: inconclusive,
  document and archive.

## How to run this forward test

Step 1: Accumulate fresh sessions. Each trading day after May 12 adds
one session. Pull GEX, ES bars, and MBP-10 data as per the data
refresh scripts already in the repo.

Step 2: Every 10 new sessions, run a pulse check (not a verdict):
  - Run full OMEN backtest on all accumulated fresh sessions
  - Tag each trade with cell
  - Report: N trades per cell, per-cell Sharpe, cumulative direction
  - Do NOT declare verdict until minimum sample sizes are met
  - Do NOT adjust parameters based on pulse checks

Step 3: At 30 fresh sessions, run the formal verdict check per
decision criteria above for Hypothesis 1.

Step 4: At 30 LS-specific trades (estimated ~52 sessions), run the
formal verdict check for Hypothesis 2.

Step 5: Accept the verdicts. Do not adjust criteria after seeing data.
